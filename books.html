may

  • Finna - Nino Cipri

    eh! luckily it was a novella, otherwise i'm not sure it would have held my interest for that long. it's interesting seeing diversity in books, but there's something about presenting it in such a relevant, millennial way that makes it feels forced or artificial. of course a nonbinary character would have to deal with their boss misgendering them, and of course the mc has depression and anxiety, but somehow putting it all out there makes it feels unnatural. maybe it's too real. maybe it's too anchored in reality (ironic considering it has wormholes and alternate dimensions of ikea). i'd say what i found weird about the book is that's probably aimed more towards a ya audience, as at moments it did remind me of the ya book i accidentally started reading. there's something just too obvious about it, no matter where the story goes. it does have poetic moments near the end, and we obviously see character growth in everyone involved, but there's something very lifeless and soulless about the book. sorry! might check out the sequel in the future.

  • Baby Teeth - Zoje Stage

    boy, what a pain in the ass!
    so long, so not worth the wait. i'm very confused about what this is trying to say about mental health or children or parents or white people. maybe it's just a dumb story, but it feels like it's going a true crime route and is definitely villanizing aspd. wasn't bothered by the kid's voice and i could very well believe everything that happened, but just not worth the read.

  • Dear Child - Romy Hausmann

    so fucking boring. maybe it gets better but i couldn't get past the first couple of chapters. horrible choice after dealing with the child's perspective in babyteeth. turned me off books for the rest of the month
